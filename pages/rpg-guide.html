SImps TTRPG — How to Play

Rules, categories, stat blocks, and the taxonomy of a universe that defies sensible classification.

Reference Rules

What Is SImps?

SImps is an improv-adjacent tabletop RPG universe built from years of shows, bits, characters, and collaborative nonsense. The wiki documents this universe: every character who has appeared, every faction that has formed, every arena that has been used or theorized about. It is both a reference document and a continuity emergency.

The game runs on vibes, escalating commitment, and the collective memory of everyone who has ever watched or performed a SImps set. There are rules below. The rules are suggestions. The power ranking is law.

Core Rules

1. The Power Ranking Is Canonical

The power ranking determines who wins a direct confrontation when all else is equal. If #7 fights #34, #7 wins unless the fiction provides a compelling reason otherwise. The GM can override rankings for dramatic purposes, but the override must be narratively earned. "I just feel like it" does not override the ranking.

2. Context Changes Everything

The ranking reflects general power. Specific contexts shift outcomes. Detective Steven ranks #5 only in Christmas-saving mode. Tommy the Rattlesnake ranks #31 only if Mirage is real. Always check the entry page for context modifiers before running an encounter.

3. Stat Blocks Are Guidance, Not Gospel

Every entry has a stat block with six stats: PWR, CHO, MEN, VIB, WIT, and BIT. These are generated deterministically from the entry's slug and adjusted by category and rank. They provide a framework. Edit them on any entry page if the canon requires different numbers — changes sync to all visitors.

4. Gangs Act As One

When a faction enters an encounter, use the highest individual rank within that faction as the effective rank. Gang members contribute their stat bonuses collectively. The Volcano 4 Beings fighting together use #13 (Tony Stony) as their floor and combine punch, stare, stomp, and dance outputs.

5. Arena Matters

Arenas are not neutral. Running a fight in the Water Goblin Kingdom (Underwater) disadvantages anything that can't breathe water. The Apple Police arena is home turf for law enforcement entities. When rolling an arena, treat its environmental properties as active constraints, not decoration.

6. Community Entries Are Canon Until Voted Out

Entries added via the "+ Add Entry" button are immediately part of the wiki and can affect the power ranking. Anyone can add, edit, or delete community entries. Community entries display a "Community added" badge. If the table disagrees with a community entry, resolve it in-session and then update the wiki to match.

The Six Stats

Every entity in the wiki has six stats ranging from 1–20, plus a modifier calculated as (stat − 10) ÷ 2, rounded down.

PWR — Power Raw physical force, strength, and direct combat capability. High PWR wins slugfests.
CHO — Chaos Unpredictability, disruption, and entropy generation. High CHO means the encounter goes sideways.
MEN — Menace Threat presence, psychological weight, and the degree to which the enemy regrets showing up.
VIB — Vibe Aesthetic authority, crowd energy, and social terrain control. High VIB wins rooms before fights start.
WIT — Wit Intelligence, planning, and the ability to notice things others miss. High WIT wins long games.
BIT — Bit Comedy value, narrative significance, and how memorable the encounter will be. BIT does not help you win. BIT makes it worth watching.

Power Tiers

The 119 ranked characters are divided into five tiers. Tier determines encounter difficulty scaling and NPC attitude. Below-tier enemies show deference; above-tier enemies treat the encounter as unserious.

TierRanksDescription
Mythic#1–10Gods, world-scale entities, and the structurally important. Encounters are set pieces, not fights.
Legendary#11–25The genuinely dangerous. Victory is possible but costs something. The party will remember it.
Epic#26–50Formidable opponents. Standard adventuring tier — the kind of encounter the game is designed around.
Elite#51–80Competent and motivated. Not pushover material. Run well, they punch above their tier.
Standard#81–119Still ranked. Still in the wiki. The floor of "something worth documenting."

Running an Encounter

  1. Roll or choose an arena using the Roll an arena button on the home page. Apply its environmental rules.
  2. Roll or choose a character using Roll a character. Check their power tier and context modifiers.
  3. Compare stat blocks. The entity with the higher relevant stat wins the stat contest — but the GM describes how.
  4. BIT determines how the scene is remembered. High BIT = canonically funny. Low BIT = just a fight.
  5. Update the wiki after the session. Add lore to entry pages. Note what happened in GM notes. The wiki is a living document.
